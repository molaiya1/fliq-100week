## 1. Project Overview & Brand Context

### What Is The $100 Week™?

The $100 Week™ is a free, browser-based financial decision simulation built by WealthWise Kids LLC, a financial literacy media IP company. Students receive a hypothetical $100 and navigate five consecutive daily financial decisions — each one rooted in real spending pressure, peer influence, and opportunity cost. The simulation is live at **play.wealthwiselearning.com** and used by schools, youth programs, and community organizations nationwide.

The simulation is not a classroom worksheet. It is a *cinematic experience*. Each of the five daily decision screens features a full-bleed illustrated background that places the student inside the scene — a school book fair, a sneaker boutique, a broken-down bike on a quiet road. The illustrations are the emotional core of the product.

### Brand Identity — WealthWise Kids™

**Positioning:** WealthWise Kids is not a curriculum company. It is a media IP company with institutional distribution. The closest analogues are Rugrats (character-driven, emotionally authentic, kid-respecting) and Sesame Street (institutional trust, evidence-grounded, lasting cultural presence).

**Tone:** Vibrant, warm, optimistic. Real but never gritty. Hopeful but never saccharine. These kids deal with real financial pressure — peer pressure, limited resources, competing priorities — and the visuals must honor that reality without moralizing. Think a Pixar short, not a financial literacy pamphlet.

**Brand Colors:**

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Body Navy | `#1F3C88` | Primary brand color — text overlays, UI chrome |
| Cover Navy | `#242E67` | Cover pages, hero headers only |
| Gold | `#F4B223` | Accent, Milo's eyes, CTA elements |
| Warm Cream | `#FFF8EC` | Light accent, card backgrounds |

The Body Navy and Gold should feel *present* in every scene — not applied artificially, but embedded naturally in props, signage, clothing, or ambient lighting. These scenes should feel like they already belong to the WealthWise Kids universe before the UI is ever overlaid.

### How the Illustrations Are Used

Each illustrated scene is rendered at 1920×1080px and displayed as a full-screen background behind a floating UI panel. The UI panel sits on the **left third** of the screen and contains the scenario text, financial data, and choice buttons. The right two-thirds of the screen is where the scene lives.

This has two direct implications for composition:
1. The **left side** of every illustration must be naturally dark or shadowed — not black, but deep enough to ensure Body Navy text remains legible over it without additional overlay tricks. Think a hallway opening onto a bright room, a storefront doorway, interior shadow falling from a window.
2. The **right side** is where the action is richest — the display, the crowd, the setting detail. This is what the student's eye is drawn to.

## 2. Character Reference Summary

### Milo Peregrine™ — Brand Mascot

Milo is a **blue great horned owl** with **gold eyes** and a **white chest**. He is warm, expressive, and curious — never condescending. He is the brand's emotional anchor and appears in every scene, but he is **never the protagonist**. He does not speak on screen. He observes, reacts, and witnesses.

**Milo's role in scenes:** Milo should appear in a natural perch, corner, or edge position within the scene — sitting on a bookshelf, perched on a sign, resting on a windowsill, tucked into the top corner of a display rack. His position should feel like he *belongs there*, not like he was placed for the viewer's benefit. His expression should match the emotional temperature of the scene (curious, a little concerned, quietly excited). His gold eyes should catch the scene's light source.

**Milo is never:**
- The center of the frame
- Speaking or gesturing toward the viewer
- Positioned in the left shadow zone (he should always be visible)
- Shown in a realistic or naturalistic owl style — he is stylized and expressive

**Reference feel for Milo:** Archimedes from The Sword in the Stone, but warmer. Hedwig, but more emotionally expressive and less regal.

---

### The Crew — Six Student Characters

The Crew are six student characters modeled after real individuals (the creator's godchildren). They appear as background figures in scenes — present, relatable, and visually diverse, but not protagonists. They are not speaking or gesturing toward the viewer. They are simply *there*, living in the moment the simulation captures.

One or two Crew members should appear in each scene. Their clothing, accessories, and body language should reflect the age band of the scene and the specific emotional stakes of the day's scenario.

**Character Reference:**

| Character | Age Band | Appearance Notes | Personality Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Miller** | Explorer–Builder | Black boy, medium build, natural hair, easy smile | Curious, the one who asks questions |
| **Sai** | Explorer–Builder | South Asian girl, long dark hair, bright eyes | Thoughtful, slightly cautious |
| **Ava** | Builder–Strategist | Black girl, locs or natural hair, expressive | Social, trend-aware, leader energy |
| **Macon** | Builder–Strategist | Mixed-race boy, athletic build, confident posture | Competitive, entrepreneurial |
| **Naiya** | Strategist | Black girl, fashion-forward, sharp |  Strategic thinker, reads the room |
| **Brooke-Lynn** | Explorer–Strategist | White girl, curly hair, artistic detail in clothing | Creative, independent, observant |

**Character placement rules:**
- No more than two Crew members per scene
- They should be positioned mid-ground or background — not in the foreground interacting with the viewer
- Body language should reflect the scene's emotional stakes (excitement, hesitation, social pressure, solo reflection) without telegraphing a moral
- Clothing should be contemporary and age-appropriate — not school uniform unless the scene explicitly calls for it, not brand-logoed

### EXPLORER BAND — Ages 8–11

#### Explorer Day 1 — School Book Fair
**Scenario context:** A student has $100 and the book fair just opened. There are books, posters, erasers, pencil sets, cool stuff — and a limited budget.

**Mood:** Excited anticipation. That specific electric energy of the first morning the book fair is open.

**Lighting:** Bright overhead classroom light, warm supplemental lighting from a lamp on the librarian cart. No dramatic shadows — this is daylight energy.

**Key scene elements:**
- Folding display tables draped in brightly colored tablecloths
- Stacked books with illustrated covers visible (no real titles — original designs)
- Spinner racks with bookmarks, erasers, posters, pencil sets
- A hand-lettered or printed price sign visible somewhere (echoes the $100 premise without stating it)
- Ceiling banners or streamers in school-fair colors
- A librarian or teacher figure in the background (do not feature — background only)
- Warm wood-toned classroom furniture visible in periphery

**Crew placement:** Miller and Sai, mid-ground, looking at a spinner rack together. Miller is pointing at something; Sai looks like she's doing mental math.

**Milo placement:** Perched on top of a bookshelf in the upper right, looking down at the spinner rack with his gold eyes catching the overhead light. Curious expression.

**Composition notes:** Left third should fall into the natural shadow of a bookshelf blocking ambient light from the windows. The richest display should be in the right-center of frame. The scene should feel crowded in a good way — like the fair is *happening*.

**Mood:** Pure after-school joy, but with a line and the awareness that money is a finite thing.

**Lighting:** Late afternoon golden hour. Long shadows on the sidewalk. Warm, saturated daylight that pushes toward the gold of the brand palette naturally.

**Key scene elements:**
- A classic push cart or truck-style ice cream cart — colorful, well-illustrated menu board visible
- A line of kids (stylized, non-specific background figures) leading to the cart
- Tree-lined suburban sidewalk, school building receding in the background
- A fire hydrant, a bike rack, a backpack on the ground — neighborhood texture
- Dappled light through leaves on the sidewalk

**Crew placement:** Ava is near the middle of the line, looking at the menu. Brooke-Lynn is next to her, already holding a cone (she already decided).

**Milo placement:** Perched on top of the ice cream cart sign, resting. He looks content but slightly bemused — like he knows something the kids don't.

**Composition notes:** Left side falls into the natural shadow of a large tree on the left edge of frame. The cart and the richest color and action should be center-right. Long shadows pull the eye right. The school building in the upper-left background provides depth and anchors the setting.

**Lighting:** Warm ambient library lighting — a mix of soft overhead fluorescents and warm accent lamps. Late afternoon light through tall windows creates a reading-nook warmth.

**Key scene elements:**

**Crew placement:** Miller is seated at a reading table in the mid-ground, looking up from a book — not at the viewer, but at something off-frame right. He looks like he's just had a thought.

**Milo placement:** Perched on a bookshelf in the upper-right, half-hidden between two books. His gold eyes are visible, watching Miller.

**Composition notes:** Left third should fall naturally into shadow from the bookshelf depth. Windows behind the right section of the scene create a rim-light effect on the shelves. Scene should feel still and warm — not empty, but quiet.

**Milo placement:** Perched high on a window ledge in the upper right corner, looking down at the scene. Expression is amused and curious — this is a lot of information.

## 4. Technical Specifications

### Dimensions & Format
- **Output resolution:** 1920×1080px minimum (delivery at 300dpi recommended for future print applications)
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 landscape — strict, no exceptions
- **Color mode:** RGB (screen delivery primary; CMYK conversion may be requested for print adaptations)
- **File formats:** Deliverables in both layered source file (PSD or equivalent) and flattened high-resolution PNG (transparent background not required — full-bleed illustrations)

### Composition Requirements — Non-Negotiable
- **Left third shadow zone:** Every scene must have a naturally dark or shadowed region covering approximately the left 30–35% of the frame. This zone must support legible white and Body Navy (`#1F3C88`) text without any additional overlay layer applied by the development team. The shadow should feel *earned by the scene*, not artificially imposed.
- **Right side action zone:** The primary setting, action, and visual richness should live in the right 60–65% of the frame.
- **Safe zones:** No critical character or scene elements should be placed within 80px of any edge (to accommodate deployment across slightly varying display sizes).

### Character Placement Rules
- Milo appears in every scene — no exceptions
- Milo must always be visible (never fully in the left shadow zone)
- Maximum two Crew members per scene
- Crew members are mid-ground or background — never in the foreground breaking the fourth wall
- No character should appear to be interacting directly with the viewer

### Color Palette Adherence
- Brand gold (`#F4B223`) should appear naturally in at least one element per scene — lighting, signage, Milo's eyes, a prop, or environmental accent
- Brand navy (`#1F3C88`) may appear in signage, clothing, or environmental elements — subtly, not artificially
- Overall palette should feel warm and vibrant. Avoid desaturated or muted treatments. These are not editorial illustrations — they should feel alive.

## 5. Art Direction & Style References

### Primary Style Target: Semi-Realistic Illustration
The style should sit between the warmth of Pixar feature films and the grounded visual texture of a high-quality animated series. Think *Soul* or *Turning Red* — not *Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse* (too stylized) and not *My Little Pony* (too flat/graphic).

**What this means in practice:**
- Lighting is volumetric — sources create depth, cast shadows, and bounce
- Environments have texture — wood grain, fabric folds, concrete imperfections
- Characters are stylized but anatomically grounded — expressive faces, realistic proportions with slight stylization
- Color is rich and saturated but not candy-colored — natural tones anchored by the brand palette
- Backgrounds have depth — foreground, mid-ground, background planes are all populated

### What to Avoid
- **Flat design / vector art:** These scenes require depth and lighting. Flat illustration reads as infographic, not story.
- **Clipart aesthetic:** Props and settings should feel drawn, not assembled.
- **Stock photo collage:** Every element should be original illustration.
- **Overly textbook-y:** No sterile, generic "educational content" visual language. This is media, not curriculum.
- **Corporate clean:** A little visual mess (a dropped backpack, an uneven stack of books) makes scenes feel real.

### Style References (Mood Boards)
The following references are provided for directional guidance — not for recreation:

| Reference | What to Borrow |
|---|---|
| *Pixar's Soul* (2020) | Urban environment warmth, lighting depth, character expressiveness in non-human figure (Milo) |
| *Turning Red* (2022) | Warm suburban Canadian setting energy, the specificity of a middle-school social world, warm color temperature |
| *The Proud Family: Louder & Prouder* (2022) | Black character design warmth and cultural authenticity, neighborhood texture |
| *Bluey* (2018) | Character emotional expressiveness, everyday-setting richness, the ability to make a living room feel cinematic |
| *Rugrats* (1991–2004) | Character-first world-building, kid perspective on everyday settings — things that adults walk past, kids notice |

The illustrator or studio should internalize these references and synthesize, not copy. The WealthWise Kids visual world is its own — these references indicate the *quality level* and *emotional temperature*, not the specific visual style.

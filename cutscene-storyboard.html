The Visual Throughline — The Coin Bag

Every clip in this series must feature the coin bag — the student's simulated wallet. This single object appears in all 6 clips and is the thread that makes the sequence feel like one story rather than six separate animations. It starts full of possibility in Clip 1, loses money in Clip 3, appears in both states at the finish in Clip 5, and is sealed with the FLIQ Score™ lock in Clip 6. The bag is how the student sees themselves across the whole week.

Emotional Target

Weight and momentum. Final lap energy — not coasting, sprinting. The two students walking side by side communicates: different paths, same commitment.

👜 Coin Bag Throughline

Two bags, two weights, one finish line. This is the payoff of tracking the bag across the whole series. One is full, one is lighter — but both students are still in the game. Neither is disqualified. The message: how you finish is what matters, not how much you have. The bags must look visibly different from each other.

Text Rule

Emotional Target

Celebration. Anticipation. Pride. The student earned this. The clip plays while their FLIQ Score™ is being calculated — it must build enough energy to make the result feel like a reveal.

👜 Coin Bag Throughline — CLOSE

The same coin bag from Clip 1 returns here. It should be visually recognizable as the same object — but now it carries a glowing lock labeled "FLIQ Score™". The lock opens with a click. Gold light pulses outward. This is the payoff of the entire story — the bag the student has been tracking all week finally reveals what's inside. The story ends where it began.

Text Rule
"Your results are ready."

Playfair Display, italic, white, centered. Appears after the gold pulse. Hold 1.5s, then fade to black. This and Clip 1 are the only two clips with any text — and both are ceremonial, not instructional.

Milo
  • Center of frame — this is his earned moment
  • Full celebratory spin — the only time he moves this much
  • Energy up — but still him. Not over-the-top
  • He carried the student through all 5 days. Let him celebrate

Facilitation Insight 05
Behavioral Interpretation · Generated from Session B7K2
Your class can plan — but social pressure breaks their execution.
The most consistent signal from this session is a gap between stated intent and observed behavior under social pressure. On Day 3 (unexpected expense) and Day 5 (final choice), students made strong, forward-looking decisions — 61% and 70% respectively chose the financially disciplined path. But when the group was watching on Day 1 and Day 2, discipline dropped sharply. This is not a knowledge gap. It is a knowing-doing gap — and it is the most common pattern we see in this age band.
The Day 4 risk data adds an important nuance: students who were already low on funds took the 50/50 bet at a higher rate than students with comfortable balances. This is the "nothing to lose" risk pattern — and it directly mirrors how adults fall into debt cycles. Naming this pattern explicitly in your debrief is the highest-leverage conversation you can have.
Suggested Debrief Prompts
On Day 2, nearly half the class spent money even when they were already low. What makes it hard to say no to the group — even when you know you should?
Some students in the class bet on Day 4 when they could least afford to lose. Why do you think that happens — in the game, and in real life?
Your class finished strong on Day 5 — most of you saved or set a goal. What would it look like to carry that same energy into Day 1 next time?
